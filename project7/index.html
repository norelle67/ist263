Website Purpose

In response to recent state and federal rollbacks of Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) policies, this website serves as a platform at the intersection of advocacy, empowerment, and technology. As a Black woman pursuing a degree in Information Management and Technology with a concentration in Information Security, I aim to share my experiences navigating these political changes while encouraging others to stand against systemic erasure.

This site highlights my leadership and academic experiences, including work with organizations such as BLISTS, and promotes resources for students who have been disadvantaged by anti-DEI policies. My goal is to create a digitally accessible, politically aware space that supports equity and inclusion in the technology field.

Resources for Underrepresented Communities in Tech

  • Black and Latinx Information Science and Technology Society (BLISTS) – A Syracuse University organization that supports Black and Latinx students in information science and technology through community, leadership, and professional development.
  • Girls Who Code – A national nonprofit organization working to close the gender gap in technology by providing coding education and career pathways for women and nonbinary individuals.
  • Management Leadership for Tomorrow (MLT) – An organization dedicated to increasing racial equity in career advancement by providing coaching, education, and access to professional networks.
  • The Hidden Genius Project – A nonprofit organization that trains and mentors Black male youth in technology skills, entrepreneurship, and leadership.
  • National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE) – A national organization that supports Black engineers and technologists through academic support, professional development, and career opportunities.
